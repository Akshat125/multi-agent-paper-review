## Summary
The paper introduces VideoWebArena, a novel benchmark for evaluating the capabilities of long-context multimodal agents in video understanding. The benchmark consists of 2,021 web agent tasks based on manually crafted video tutorials, totaling almost four hours of content. The tasks are divided into two main areas of focus: skill retention and factual retention. The authors evaluate several state-of-the-art models on the benchmark and find that while these models can serve as video-capable agents, they are still far from human levels of performance.

## Strengths
The paper presents a well-structured and comprehensive benchmark for evaluating long-context multimodal agents. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the benchmark, including the task taxonomy, environment, and evaluation metrics. The use of manually crafted video tutorials and a wide range of task types makes the benchmark more realistic and challenging. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the results, highlighting the strengths and weaknesses of the baseline models.

## Weaknesses
One of the main weaknesses of the paper is the limited evaluation of the baseline models. The authors only test a few models on the benchmark, and the results are not comprehensive. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the failure cases and the reasons behind the poor performance of the models. The authors also mention that the tutorials introduce negative noise that hurts action selection, but they do not provide a clear explanation for this phenomenon.

## Questions
What are the potential reasons behind the poor performance of the models on the skill retention tasks, and how can this be improved? How can the authors improve the evaluation metrics to better capture the performance of the models? What are the potential applications of the VideoWebArena benchmark, and how can it be used to improve the development of long-context multimodal agents?

RATING: 8
