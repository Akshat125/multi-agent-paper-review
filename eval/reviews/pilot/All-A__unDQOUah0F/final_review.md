## Summary  
The paper introduces VideoWebArena (VideoWA), a benchmark for evaluating the agentic capabilities of long-context multimodal models in tasks involving video understanding. It defines two main task categories: skill retention (learning from video demonstrations to perform tasks efficiently) and factual retention (extracting specific information from videos). The benchmark includes 2,021 tasks across six domains (e.g., Reddit, Shopping) and 74 manually crafted video tutorials totaling nearly four hours. The authors evaluate GPT-4o and Gemini 1.5 Pro using three baseline agent setups (video in-context, video frames in-context, and video summary in-context) and compare their performance to human benchmarks. Results show significant gaps between models and humans, with the best model achieving 13.3% success on factual retention tasks and 45.8% on intermediate QA pairs, versus 73.9% and 79.3% for humans. The paper highlights the need for improved agentic reasoning in long-context video models and provides a testbed for future research.  

## Strengths  
- **Novelty in Task Design**: VideoWA introduces a taxonomy of video-based agent tasks (skill and factual retention) that addresses a critical gap in existing benchmarks, which often focus on text or static images. The distinction between these categories is well-motivated and reflects real-world use cases, such as learning workflows from tutorials or retrieving incidental details from videos.  
- **Comprehensive Benchmark Coverage**: The benchmark includes 2,021 tasks across six diverse domains, with a detailed breakdown of task types and difficulties. The inclusion of 74 manually crafted video tutorials adds a unique, realistic component to the evaluation.  
- **Insightful Results**: The results reveal meaningful limitations in current models, such as the performance drop when tutorials are included (e.g., 5% in WebArena and 10.3% in VisualWebArena). This suggests that video input introduces noise or challenges in action selection, a novel observation for the field.  
- **Open-Source Availability**: The authors make the benchmark, code, and videos publicly available, which is a strong contribution to the community. This facilitates reproducibility and future research on long-context multimodal agents.  
- **Clear Evaluation Framework**: The use of intermediate intent evaluation allows the authors to disentangle video understanding from agentic planning, providing a more granular analysis of model failure modes.  

## Weaknesses  
- **Limited Methodological Detail**: The task creation process, criteria for difficulty ratings, and evaluation procedures are not sufficiently detailed. For example, the paper does not clarify how intent templates were selected, how video difficulty is operationalized, or how human evaluations were conducted. This hinders reproducibility and makes it difficult to assess the benchmark's robustness.  
- **Small and Biased Human Sample**: Human performance is based on only three authors attempting the tasks, which is a small and potentially biased sample. This limits the reliability of the human baseline and the interpretation of the model-human performance gap.  
- **Insufficient Model Diversity**: The evaluation includes only two models (GPT-4o and Gemini 1.5 Pro), which is not enough to generalize the findings. Including additional state-of-the-art models like LLaVA-Next or LongVILA would strengthen the paper's contribution.  
- **Binary Evaluation Metrics**: The use of binary success/failure metrics for both final tasks and intermediate intents lacks nuance. A step-wise accuracy or partial credit system would better capture the complexity of agentic reasoning and video understanding.  
- **Missing Statistical Analysis**: The paper does not report statistical significance for the observed performance differences between models or between with/without tutorials. This weakens the confidence in the conclusions drawn from the results.  
- **Ambiguity in Baseline Implementation**: The exact methods for video processing (e.g., frame sampling, summary generation) and model prompting are not clearly described. This makes it challenging to reproduce the experiments or assess the validity of the baselines.  

## Questions  
- How were the intent templates selected from WebArena and VisualWebArena, and what criteria were used to ensure diversity in the 2,021 tasks?  
- What specific steps were taken to validate the accuracy of the video summaries and intermediate intent answers, and how were hallucinations or errors in these components addressed?  
- Could the performance degradation when tutorials are included be due to the models being overwhelmed by the additional context, or is it a result of poor integration of video information into the agent's decision-making process?  
- How were the human participants selected for the evaluation, and were they naive to the task design or familiar with the domains (e.g., experienced with GitLab or online shopping)?  
- What is the exact frame sampling method used for the video frames in-context agent, and was it optimized for task performance (e.g., keyframe selection, uniform sampling)?  
- How does the Set-of-Marks framework handle dynamic or JavaScript-heavy web interactions, and are there limitations in the action space that could affect the realism of the benchmark?  

RATING: 7
