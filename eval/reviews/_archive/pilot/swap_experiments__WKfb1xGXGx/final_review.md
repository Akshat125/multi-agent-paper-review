## Summary
The paper introduces \textsc{Perm}, a parametric model for 3D hair modeling that disentangles global hair shape and local strand details using a PCA-based strand representation in the frequency domain. This approach allows for precise editing and control over hair models, addressing the biscale nature of hair and the limitations of task-specific models. The model is evaluated on tasks such as 3D hair parameterization, hairstyle interpolation, and single-view hair reconstruction, demonstrating performance equivalent or superior to state-of-the-art methods. The paper also highlights a novel application of using \textsc{Perm}-generated hair for conditional image generation.

## Strengths
- The paper presents a novel and effective approach to 3D hair modeling by disentangling global and local features using a PCA-based representation in the frequency domain. This is a significant departure from previous methods that often model these aspects jointly or use VAEs for strand latent spaces.
- The frequency domain PCA formulation provides an \emph{explainable} parameter space, where low-frequency components capture global shape and high-frequency components encode local details like curliness. This interpretability is a major advantage over black-box deep learning models.
- The modular design of \textsc{Perm}, using different generative models (StyleGAN2 for guide textures, VAE for residual textures, and U-Net for super-resolution), allows it to serve as a generic prior for various hair-related tasks. This flexibility is demonstrated through experiments on 3D hair parameterization, hairstyle interpolation, and single-view hair reconstruction.
- The paper provides a detailed comparison with GroomGen, a relevant previous work, and shows that the PCA-based strand representation outperforms the VAE-based approach in terms of position and curvature errors. The qualitative results also suggest that the synthesized hairstyles are more natural.
- The applications section showcases the practical utility of \textsc{Perm}, including its use in conditional image generation, which could have implications for portrait generation and digital human creation.

## Weaknesses
- The paper lacks concrete details on the implementation of the PCA-based strand representation, particularly how the DFT is applied to hair strands and how the inverse DFT is used to reconstruct them. A more detailed algorithm or pseudocode would improve clarity and reproducibility.
- The decomposition of hair geometry textures into guide and residual textures is not clearly explained. The paper does not specify whether this is a simple truncation of PCA coefficients or a more complex filtering or projection step.
- The paper does not provide a detailed description of the neural network architectures used for the different modules (e.g., StyleGAN2, VAE, U-Net), including layer configurations, activation functions, and normalization techniques. This omission makes it difficult to fully understand the model's design and implementation.
- The loss functions used in the training process are not clearly defined. For example, the geometric loss $\mathcal{L}_{\text{geo}}$ is described in terms of point positions, orientations, and curvatures, but the exact computation of these components is not explained. The weighting factors for the losses are provided, but the reasoning behind their selection is not discussed.
- The paper relies heavily on the supplemental material for key figures and results, which are not included in the main text. This makes the paper less self-contained and harder to follow without access to the supplemental.
- The comparison with GroomGen is limited, as the paper does not provide a detailed description of their implementation of GroomGen. This lack of transparency could affect the fairness of the comparison.
- The paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the model's limitations, particularly in handling complex hairstyles like buns and braids. It is unclear whether these limitations are due to the model's architecture or the lack of training data.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the trade-offs between different generative models (e.g., VAE vs. StyleGAN2) in the context of hair modeling. A more thorough discussion of these trade-offs would help to justify the model's design choices.

## Questions
- Could the authors provide a detailed algorithm or pseudocode for the PCA-based strand representation in the frequency domain, including the exact steps for computing the DFT and iDFT?
- How is the decomposition of hair geometry textures into guide and residual textures performed? Is it a simple truncation of PCA coefficients, or is there a more complex filtering or projection step involved?
- Could the authors provide a table or figure showing the cumulative variance explained by the first 10 and the full 64 PCA components? This would help to justify the split between global and local features.
- What are the exact layer configurations, activation functions, and normalization techniques used in the StyleGAN2, VAE, and U-Net models? A diagram or table summarizing these architectures would improve clarity.
- How is the geometric loss $\mathcal{L}_{\text{geo}}$ computed? The paper mentions point positions, orientations, and curvatures, but the exact extraction and computation of these components from 3D strands is not explained.
- Could the authors provide a detailed description of the data preprocessing and augmentation steps used for the USC-HairSalon dataset? This would help in understanding how the hair models are represented and aligned.
- What is the reasoning behind the choice of weighting factors for the loss functions (e.g., $\lambda_{\text{tex}} = 1$, $\lambda_{\text{geo}} = 1$, $\lambda_{\text{reg}} = 0.1$)? A more detailed breakdown of the loss components and their roles would improve clarity.
- Could the authors expand the limitations section to include a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the PCA-based approach, particularly in handling hair with irregular or non-periodic patterns?
- How does the model's disentanglement of global and local features affect the performance of downstream tasks like hair animation or simulation? A more in-depth discussion of the model's impact on the field would strengthen the paper's contribution.

RATING: 8
