## Summary
The paper introduces VideoWebArena, a benchmark for evaluating the capabilities of long-context multimodal agents for video understanding. The benchmark consists of 2,021 web agent tasks based on manually crafted video tutorials, totaling almost four hours of content. The tasks are divided into two main areas of focus: skill retention and factual retention. The authors evaluate several video-capable state-of-the-art LLMs on the benchmark and find that while these models can serve as video-capable agents, they are still far from human levels of performance.

## Strengths
The paper presents a novel and comprehensive benchmark for evaluating the agentic abilities of long-context multimodal models. The benchmark covers a wide range of task types, including skill retention and factual retention, and provides a rigorous test bed for the setting of video agents. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the results, highlighting the strengths and weaknesses of the baseline agents. The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the benchmark, tasks, and results.

## Weaknesses
One of the main weaknesses of the paper is the limited evaluation of the baseline agents. The authors only test a few models on the benchmark, and the results are not comprehensive. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the failure cases and the reasons behind the poor performance of the baseline agents. The authors also mention that the tutorials introduce negative noise that hurts action selection, but they do not provide a clear explanation of why this is the case. Furthermore, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other related work in the field.

## Questions
What are the main reasons behind the poor performance of the baseline agents on the benchmark? How can the tutorials be improved to avoid introducing negative noise that hurts action selection? What are the potential applications of the VideoWebArena benchmark, and how can it be used to improve the development of long-context multimodal agents? How does the performance of the baseline agents compare to other state-of-the-art models in the field, and what are the implications of the results for the development of more advanced video-capable agents?

RATING: 8
