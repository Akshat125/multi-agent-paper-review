## Summary  
The paper introduces **IgSeek**, a novel structure-retrieval framework for antibody design that addresses the challenge of hallucinations in sequence inference for Complementarity-Determining Regions (CDRs). Unlike traditional generative models, IgSeek leverages a database of natural antibody structures to retrieve structurally similar CDRs and uses their conserved sequence motifs to infer the target sequence. The method employs a **Multi-channel Equivariant Graph Neural Network (MEGNN)** to generate geometric representations of CDR backbone structures, ensuring invariance to 3D transformations. Experiments show that IgSeek outperforms state-of-the-art methods in sequence recovery accuracy and inference speed, particularly for hyper-variable CDR-H3 loops. The paper also demonstrates generalization to T-Cell Receptor (TCR) data and highlights the efficiency of the approach for high-throughput design tasks.

## Strengths  
The paper presents a **novel and effective retrieval-based approach** to antibody CDR sequence design, which is a significant departure from existing generative models. The use of MEGNN to encode CDR structures with E(3) equivariance is a strong technical contribution, as it ensures robustness to spatial transformations. The experiments are **comprehensive and well-structured**, comparing IgSeek against multiple state-of-the-art models (e.g., FoldSeek, ProteinMPNN, AbMPNN, AntiFold) on diverse datasets, including both solved and predicted structures. The results show **notable improvements in sequence recovery accuracy**, with at least a 2.9% increase in AAR for light chain CDRs and a 30% improvement for TCR data. The **efficiency of IgSeek is also a major strength**, with a 20x speed-up in inference time compared to baselines, which is critical for practical applications. The case study on the PDB 8W8R CDR-L1 loop and the T-SNE visualization of CDR embeddings further support the method's effectiveness in capturing structural relationships.

## Weaknesses  
While the paper makes a strong case for the efficacy of IgSeek, there are **several limitations and concerns** that need to be addressed. First, the **lack of explicit ablation studies** makes it difficult to assess the individual contributions of the MEGNN components (e.g., multi-channel design, equivariance, self-supervised training). The authors rely on a visualization and a comparison with FoldSeek to justify their approach, but a more detailed analysis of how each architectural choice impacts performance would strengthen the paper. Second, the **reproducibility of the results is compromised** by the absence of key implementation details, such as hyperparameters, software versions, and hardware specifications. The paper also does not mention whether the code or data will be publicly available, which is essential for independent validation. Third, the **motivation for the retrieval-based approach** is not fully developed in the introduction. While the paper references hallucinations in generative models, it could better explain why retrieval is a more suitable solution for CDRs, especially given their structural and sequence diversity. Finally, the **comparison with AntiFold and AbMPNN is somewhat limited**, as the authors note a performance degradation in these models under their specific setup. A more in-depth discussion of the reasons for this discrepancy and how IgSeek's design mitigates these issues would improve the paper's clarity and impact.

## Questions  
1. Could the authors provide a detailed ablation study to quantify the contribution of each component of the MEGNN (e.g., multi-channel design, equivariance, self-supervised training) to the overall performance of IgSeek?  
2. What specific hyperparameters and implementation details (e.g., learning rates, optimizer settings, batch sizes) were used during the training of the MEGNN encoder?  
3. Will the authors make the code and datasets used in the experiments publicly available, and if so, under what license or access conditions?  
4. How does the performance of IgSeek change when the CDR vector database is expanded to include more diverse or non-canonical CDR structures?  
5. What are the specific reasons for the performance degradation of AntiFold and AbMPNN in the SAbDab-2024 dataset compared to their reported results in the literature?
