## Summary
The paper "SciLitLLM: How to Adapt LLMs for Scientific Literature Understanding" proposes a novel hybrid strategy that integrates continual pre-training (CPT) and supervised fine-tuning (SFT) to adapt Large Language Models (LLMs) for scientific literature understanding. The authors provide a detailed description of their methodology, which includes a pipeline for constructing high-quality domain corpora for CPT and generating diverse scientific instructions for SFT. They evaluate their approach on several benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of their strategy in improving the performance of LLMs on scientific literature understanding tasks. The paper also discusses the limitations of their approach and provides suggestions for future work.

## Strengths
One of the main strengths of the paper is its clear and well-structured methodology, which provides a comprehensive overview of the proposed hybrid CPT-SFT pipeline. The authors also conduct robust ablation studies, providing insights into the components of their pipeline and demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach. Additionally, the paper presents a novel instruction synthesis method, which is a significant contribution to the field. The results of the experiments are well-presented, and the authors provide a clear discussion of the limitations of their approach and suggestions for future work.

## Weaknesses
One of the main weaknesses of the paper is the lack of detail in certain areas, such as the format and grammar correction, quality filtering criteria, and hyperparameter tuning. The authors could benefit from providing more information on these aspects to enhance the reproducibility of their approach. Additionally, the choice of baselines could be more robust and representative, and the ablation studies could be improved with more detailed analysis and quantification of the marginal gains of CPT and SFT. The paper could also benefit from more examples or visualizations of the high-quality CPT texts and SFT instructions, as well as a more nuanced interpretation of the results in the conclusion.

## Questions
To further assess the paper, I would like the authors to address the following questions: (1) Can you provide more detail on the format and grammar correction, quality filtering criteria, and hyperparameter tuning used in your approach? (2) How do you plan to address the limitations of your approach, such as the lack of reasoning enhancement and preference alignment? (3) Can you provide more examples or visualizations of the high-quality CPT texts and SFT instructions to demonstrate the effectiveness of your instruction synthesis method? (4) How do you think your approach could be adapted to other specialized domains, and what are the potential challenges and limitations of such an adaptation? (5) Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the results, including a discussion of why SciLitLLM performs better on certain tasks and what are the implications of these results for the field of scientific literature understanding?
